A Brief History of the Internet

What is the internet?

The internet means many things to different people. It is communication, connections, e-commerce, computers, and information sharing through connecting computers from different locations across the globe. According to Chard Hurley, "Internet is connecting individuals to information." (check content for more information)

Where did the internet come from?

The idea of the internet came about from the impact of the cold war on the communication system. To create an indestructible and more efficient communication system, J.C.R Licklider came up with an idea time-sharing idea. This idea led to the creation of ARPANET, the forerunner of today's internet. (link more in the content)

Who invented the internet?

One person did not invent the internet. It evolved through the years by the collaboration and contribution of people around the world. Check out the people and invention page for more information.

Who controls the internet?

No one and everyone controls the internet. The internet is made up of independently operated networks. Hence, in the future, continued infrastructure growth because of the lack of internet monopoly.

How the world is changing the web?

The internet being a space where anyone can input and contribute knowledge sharing made its expansion incredible over a short period. This inclusion made people come up with how to make the web relevant for everyday life. This invention has made the internet's use go beyond transferring information to a lot more. Now the internet is used for online shopping, casinos, accessing transportation, banking, online cinemas, and more.
